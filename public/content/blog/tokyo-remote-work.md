Nov 2020 - Sep 2021. Sony Creative AI Lab. Tokyo. From my apartment in Taiwan.

## What I Expected

- Time zone chaos (JST +1 hour from Taipei—not bad)
- Isolation from team
- Limited mentorship
- Slowed collaboration

## What Actually Happened

The time zone was a non-issue. 9 AM Taipei = 10 AM Tokyo. Easy.

**The real challenge:** Async communication culture clash.

### Taiwan (My Default Mode)
- Quick Slack messages
- Expect responses within minutes
- Jump on calls freely

### Japan (Sony's Culture)
- Detailed emails
- Responses within hours/days (thoughtful, thorough)
- Meetings are scheduled, structured, purposeful

First month: frustration. "Why does everything take so long?"

Second month: appreciation. "Oh, this forces me to think through questions before asking."

## What Worked Well

**Weekly sync meetings** with my mentor:
- 60 minutes, camera on
- First 30 min: progress review, blockers
- Last 30 min: open technical discussion

This structure prevented the "remote drift" where you stop knowing what your team cares about.

**Shared Jupyter notebooks** (Google Colab):
- I'd prototype during Taipei hours
- Mentor would review + add comments during Tokyo hours
- Async code review that actually worked

**Demo videos instead of live demos:**
- Record 3-5 minute demos of work in progress
- Team watches async
- Feedback via timestamped comments

Honestly better than live demos. No pressure, can re-record if needed.

## What I Missed

**Hallway conversations.** The unplanned kind where someone says "hey, weird idea..." and two hours later you've prototyped something unhinged.

Remote work optimizes for **planned collaboration**. The unplanned stuff—the stuff that leads to DJTransGAN existing in the first place—requires intentional effort.

We tried "virtual coffee chats" but they felt forced. Never found a good solution.

## Would I Do It Again?

Yes. But hybrid > fully remote.

**Ideal split:**
- 3 days remote (deep work, coding, writing)
- 2 days in-office (brainstorming, debugging together, random collisions)

Fully remote works for execution. Struggles with exploration.

## Advice for Remote Interns

1. **Over-communicate progress.** No one can see you working. Make work visible.
2. **Ask questions in public channels.** Others might have the same question. Plus, knowledge gets documented.
3. **Record everything.** Screen recordings > live demos. Async > sync.
4. **Set boundaries.** "Always online" ≠ productive. Block deep work time.
5. **Travel if possible.** I visited Tokyo for the last two weeks. Met the team. Changed everything.

That final in-person sprint made the previous 10 months make sense. Faces to names. Jokes landed differently. Trust built faster.

**Remote first, but not remote only.**
