A modular toolkit for designing customizable flute-like instruments, enabling both traditional replication and novel designs via 3D printing.

## Overview

FlueBricks emerged from research at the Computational Physicality Lab at National Taiwan University. The project explores how digital fabrication can democratize musical instrument design, allowing musicians and makers to prototype custom wind instruments rapidly.

## Technical Approach

The toolkit combines:
- **Fusion 360** for parametric CAD modeling
- **3D printing** for rapid physical prototyping
- **Python-based evaluation systems** for acoustic analysis
- **Arduino** for sensor integration and testing

## Research Questions

How can we make instrument design accessible to non-experts while maintaining acoustic quality? Can modular design enable both faithful replicas of traditional instruments and entirely novel creations?

## Current Status

The system has been used to prototype multiple flute variants, with acoustic measurements showing comparable performance to traditional instruments in certain frequency ranges.
