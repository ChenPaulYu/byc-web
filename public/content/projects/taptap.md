An interactive interface using O-KAI SINGERS' a cappella samples and Tone.js. Exhibited at Vocal Asia Festival, Japan.

## Concept

What if vocal samples could be instruments? Taptap explores this idea by turning a cappella recordings into playable, tap-responsive sound modules.

## Implementation

- **Sample library**: High-quality vocal recordings from O-KAI SINGERS
- **Tone.js**: Handles timing, playback, and effects
- **Touch-optimized UI**: Responsive to both mouse and touch input
- **Real-time effects**: Pitch shifting, reverb, delay applied live

## Exhibition

Showcased at **Vocal Asia Festival** in Japan, where visitors could:
- Tap pads to trigger different vocal phrases
- Layer multiple voices rhythmically
- Create impromptu a cappella arrangements

## Reflection

The project demonstrated how familiar vocal timbres can make abstract electronic interfaces more approachable. Users who might be intimidated by synthesizers felt comfortable "playing" voices.
